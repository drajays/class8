## Olympiad / NEET Target Question (Class 8 ICSE)
**Difficulty Rating:** ICSE ★★★★★ | Olympiad / Foundation Level
**Topic:** Matter (Kinetic Theory & Diffusion in Gases)
**Format:** Single Correct Answer

### **Question**
A gas jar filled with dense, reddish-brown bromine vapor (which is significantly heavier than air) is placed below an inverted gas jar containing clear air. After some time, the reddish-brown color spreads upward, resulting in a completely uniform mixture across both jars.

Which of the following statements provides the most accurate scientific explanation of this phenomenon based on the Kinetic Theory of Matter?

* **A)** The upward movement occurs because the intermolecular forces between air and bromine molecules are highly attractive, actively pulling the heavier vapor upward.
* **B)** The gas particles possess high kinetic energy and are in constant, random motion, causing them to mix uniformly against the pull of gravity through continuous molecular collisions.
* **C)** Once the reddish-brown color becomes perfectly uniform throughout both jars, a state of static equilibrium is reached, and all molecular movement stops.
* **D)** If the temperature of both jars is significantly decreased, the rate of intermixing will increase because the denser gas will expand faster.

---

### **Answer Key & Analytical Solution**
**Correct Answer:** B

#### **Scientific Explanation:**
* **Why B is correct:** According to the Kinetic Theory of Matter, particles of a gas possess high kinetic energy and are in a state of continuous, rapid, and random motion. Even though bromine molecules are much heavier than air molecules, their thermal kinetic energy at room temperature is large enough to cause them to move in all directions. Constant collisions between the moving bromine and air molecules distribute them evenly throughout the combined space, completely overcoming the macroscopic settling effect of gravity over time. This process is called diffusion.
* **Why A is incorrect:** Intermolecular forces of attraction in gases are negligible, not strongly attractive. Diffusion is driven by random kinetic motion and concentration gradients, not by mutual attraction.
* **Why C is incorrect:** When the color becomes uniform, the system reaches dynamic equilibrium, not static equilibrium. The molecules continue to move and cross between the jars at equal rates, so there is no net change in color or concentration, but motion never stops.
* **Why D is incorrect:** Decreasing the temperature lowers the average kinetic energy of the molecules. As a result, the molecular velocity decreases, which actually slows down the rate of diffusion.

## Olympiad / NEET Target Question (Class 8 ICSE)
**Difficulty Rating:** ICSE ★★★★★ | Olympiad / Foundation Level
**Topic:** Matter (Properties & Particle Model)
**Format:** Single Correct Answer

### **Question**
Which one of the following statements is scientifically accurate regarding matter and its particles?

* **A)** Liquid wax melts at 55 °C and is converted into its liquid form.
* **B)** Matter is anything that has mass and occupies some space.
* **C)** A liquid can be compressed slightly but a gas cannot be compressed.
* **D)** All molecules of different substances are identical in size and mass.

---

### **Answer Key & Analytical Solution**
**Correct Answer:** B

#### **Scientific Explanation:**
* **Why B is correct:** This is the standard ICSE definition of matter — anything that possesses mass and occupies space (volume).
* **Why A is incorrect:** Solid wax melts at approximately 55 °C to become liquid wax. The statement incorrectly says "liquid wax melts," which is scientifically inaccurate phrasing.
* **Why C is incorrect:** This reverses the compressibility relationship. Gases are highly compressible due to large intermolecular spaces; liquids are only slightly compressible.
* **Why D is incorrect:** Molecules of different substances differ in size, mass, and structure. Only molecules of the same pure substance are identical.

*Note: "A molecule may consist of one or more atoms" is also scientifically true, but it defines a molecule — not matter itself — which is why B is the best answer in this context.*

## Olympiad / NEET Target Question (Class 8 ICSE)
**Difficulty Rating:** ICSE ★★★★★ | Olympiad / Foundation Level
**Topic:** Matter (Kinetic Theory & Change of State)
**Format:** Single Correct Answer

### **Question**
During the sublimation of a pure solid substance at its constant transition temperature, which of the following statements is scientifically accurate regarding the kinetic energy (KE) and potential energy (PE) of its molecules?

* **A)** Both the kinetic energy and the potential energy of the molecules increase simultaneously.
* **B)** The kinetic energy of the molecules increases, while their potential energy remains constant.
* **C)** The kinetic energy of the molecules remains constant, while their potential energy increases.
* **D)** The kinetic energy decreases, while the intermolecular forces of attraction become stronger.

---

### **Answer Key & Analytical Solution**
**Correct Answer:** C

#### **Scientific Explanation:**
* **Why C is correct:**
  * **Kinetic Energy (KE):** The average kinetic energy of molecules depends entirely on the temperature of the substance. Since sublimation occurs at a fixed, constant transition temperature, the kinetic energy of the molecules does not change.
  * **Potential Energy (PE):** The heat energy absorbed during sublimation (latent heat) is not used to raise the temperature. Instead, it is entirely used to overcome the strong intermolecular forces holding the solid lattice together. As intermolecular spaces increase drastically, molecular potential energy increases sharply.
* **Why A is incorrect:** If both KE and PE increased, temperature would rise — but during a change of state at constant temperature, KE is fixed.
* **Why B is incorrect:** PE cannot remain constant during sublimation; latent heat is absorbed specifically to increase molecular separation (PE).
* **Why D is incorrect:** KE does not decrease during sublimation at constant temperature; intermolecular forces are overcome (weakened in effect), not strengthened.
