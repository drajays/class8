  3. Q253. State the two absolute Laws of Optical Reflection at a boundary. [Easy]

In a uniform isotropic medium (constant speed), minimizing time means minimizing spatial distance. Utilizing the calculus of variations, the singular geometric path bouncing off a planar boundary that yields the absolute shortest total distance perfectly constraints the reflection angle to equal the incidence angle. Any asymmetric bounce is a longer, and thus forbidden, path.

  14. Q264. [HR] Why is a Convex Mirror physically constrained by the laws of optics to exclusively yield virtual, diminished, and erect images, regardless of where the object is situated? Prove via the Mirror Equation. [Hard]
    Ans: Utilizing standard Cartesian sign convention, a convex mirror's focal point sits behind the glass, so $f > 0$. A real object sits in front, making its coordinate $u < 0$. The Mirror Equation states $1/v = 1/f - 1/u$. Substituting the signs yields $1/v = 1/(|f|) + 1/(|u|)$. Because the right side is the sum of two strictly positive numbers, $1/v$ must always be positive. Therefore, $v$ is always positive (the image is permanently trapped behind the mirror as Virtual). Furthermore, mathematically, $v$ must be smaller than $|u|$, guaranteeing the magnification magnitude ($|m| = |v/u|$) is permanently less than 1 (Diminished).

  21. Q271. State Snell’s Law of Refraction and provide the mathematical definition of Absolute Refractive Index ($n$). [Easy]

  23. Q273. A laser beam transitions from atmospheric air ($n \approx 1.0$) into a dense flint glass slab ($n = 1.5$) at a steep incidence angle of 30°. Calculate the internal angle of refraction. [Medium]

From geometry, $\tan(i) = x/d$ (real depth) and $\tan(r) = x/d'$ (apparent depth). Substituting: $n \cdot (x/d) = x/d' \implies d' = d/n$.

    Ans: Optical Reversibility dictates that if a light ray reverses its vector, it will retrace its exact historical path. If an asymmetric trajectory yielded the absolute minimum deviation, shooting the ray backward along that same path must yield the same deviation, implying *two* distinct incidence angles generate the minimum. Calculus proves a single continuous function can only have one absolute minimum. Therefore, the minimum state must be its own reverse path—meaning the incidence angle must exactly equal the emergence angle ($i = e$), forcing the internal ray to travel perfectly parallel to the prism's base.
  63. Q313. A convex security mirror possesses a focal length of 15 cm. A shoplifter is positioned exactly 10 cm from the glass. Calculate the precise coordinate of their virtual image. [Medium]
