Besides education, Western models of social etiquette, dress and eating habits also began to be followed by many Indians. This led to a conflict between the traditional sections of Indian society and the pro-Western Indians.

Many British administrators and thinkers were very critical of the traditional practices and attitudes towards women prevalent in Indian society. This critique led many Indians to take a closer look at their own society and to initiate a series of reform movements.

### SOCIO-RELIGIOUS REFORM MOVEMENTS

During the 19th century, many Indian reformers emerged and advocated deliberate changes in the traditional social, cultural and religious attitudes and customs to eliminate narrow-minded practices in Indian society. These Indian reformers were the leading participants in the social and cultural revival or renaissance, which took place in the 19th century. The renaissance began in Bengal

and quickly spread to different parts of the country. It was brought on by the following factors:

Realisation of unjust and unacceptable

social customs by some Indians

Spread of scientific and rational ideas

due to Western education

Sense of pride among Indians for their past achievements

Ability of the middle class to demand reforms

### REFORM MOVEMENTS IN BENGAL

Bengal was one of the first regions to witness a series of reform movements. Let us read about some of the important reformers of this region.

##### Brahmo Samaj

Raja Ram Mohan Roy was a well-read scholar. He believed that all religions preached similar fundamental truths. He condemned priests who exploited and misled people into practising unfair customs. He fought against many evil practices in Hindu society, such as polygamy, female infanticide, rigid caste system, sati and child marriage.

Raja Ram Mohan Roy founded the Brahmo Samaj in 1828. The reforms advocated by the Brahmo Samaj under the leadership of Raja Ram Mohan Roy were as follows:

Abolition of sati and child marriage

Widow remarriage

Rejection of the caste system

Modern education for all

Equality of rights for women, and their right to property

The Brahmo Samaj was an early attempt to reform the Hindu society. It found many followers amongst educated young men who were influenced by the ideas of liberty and fraternity.

Raja Ram Mohan Roy believed that Western education could help in the spread of modern and progressive ideas. This could bring about social change. He helped to establish the Hindu College (known today as Presidency University) in Calcutta and the Vedanta College.

Raja Ram Mohan Roy worked tirelessly to reform society and regenerate feelings of nationalism among Indians. His efforts persuaded the then Governor-General, Lord William Bentinck, to pass a law banning sati, which made it a crime punishable by law.

### REFORM MOVEMENTS IN SOUTH INDIA

Kandukuri Veeresalingam was a prominent social reformer from South India. He fought for the education and upliftment of women and widow remarriage.

Sree Narayana Guru was another important spiritual and social reformer from south India. He denounced the rigid caste system prevailing in Indian society. He also stressed on the need for spiritual and social upliftment of the oppressed classes.

### SYED AHMAD KHAN AND THE ALIGARH MOVEMENT

Syed Ahmad Khan was a Muslim educator, jurist and author. He was a progressive realist and the principal motivating force behind the revival of the condition of the Indian Muslims in the late 19th century. He started the Aligarh Movement.

Syed Ahmad realised the need to promote Western education amongst Muslims. In order to achieve this, he founded the Muhammadan Anglo-Oriental College at Aligarh

The period of reform movements also witnessed considerable development in literature. Many educated Indians took to expressing their ideas and opinions in newspapers, magazines or books as poems, stories or essays. They did not write in English alone, but in their own regional languages as well. Some famous literary figures of this period were Bankim Chandra Chattopadhyay, Sarojini Naidu, Tarabai Shinde and Subramania Bharati.

### Report on Gender Stereotypes

social and cultural interaction: gender awareness

initiative and self-direction: research

critical thinking: analysis

The social reformers of the 19th century dealt with many challenges faced by women in the traditional society of that time. However, the struggle for gender equality is still relevant in the world today. In this context, make a report on the following questions. You can introspect, interview others and read articles in different media to make your report.

How and where do we learn our perception of male and female roles?

Do these roles and descriptions limit or enhance us in making life choices?

Have you or someone you know ever acted differently from how your/the other person's gender is 'supposed' to act? How?

Have you or someone you know ever stood up for a person who challenged the gender role stereotypes?
